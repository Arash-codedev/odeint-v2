The steppers can be sorted into two hierarchies, one desribes the mathematical properties of the stepper while the other one describes the steppers in terms of C++ concepts.

Steppers by concept

odeint introduces four concepts for the steppers. These concepts are describes in detail in Concepts. The most general concept is the Stepper concept which defines the basic requirements one expects on a solver of an ODE. Any stepper fulfilling this concept has to have a method do_step(sys,x,t,dt) which performs a single step. The Error Stepper concept is a simple enhancement where the do_step also computes an error estimate which is made during one step. Furthermore there exist a concept Controlled Stepper which tries to perform a step and which might accept or reject this step due to some accurrancy requirements. The fourth concept is the Dense Output Stepper which provides methods for the calculation of intermediate values during two steps.
